Dr. Alan Turing

The Man Who Revoulationized Computer Science

Timeline of Alan Turing

  • Jun 23,1912 - Born as Alan Mathison Turing in Maide Vale, London
  • Jan 1,1922 - Turing was educated at Hazelhurst Preparatory School
  • Aug 19, 1926 - Alan Turing first showed signs of intelligence and potential which was noticed at the Sherborne School.
  • Aug 29, 1931 - Alan Turing studied from 1931 to 1934 at King's College, in Cambridge.
  • Sep 30, 1936 - Studied Mathematics and Cryptology at Princeton University.
  • Dec 6, 1939 - Turing helped to solve the essential part of the German naval indicator system, Hut 8 and also came up with the idea of sequential analysis.
  • Mar 18, 1940 - Turing was responsible for creating the Bombe which was a cryptanalytic machine used to decrypt the Enigma-enciphered messages being sent from the enemies.
  • Sep 13, 1947 - Turing lived in Hampton, London where worked on the design of the ACE (Automatic Computing Engine).
  • Aug 31, 1948 - Turing was appointed a position in the mathematics department at the University of Manchester where he worked on software for one of the earliest stored-program computers.
  • Apr 29, 1950 - The turing test was created in 1950. The turing test was created to compare the intelligence of a machine/computer to the intelligence of a human brain.
  • Jun 8, 1954 -Turing committed suicide in his house where his housekeeper found him dead.
